Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our window washing services

How often should I have my windows washed?

For residential homes, we recommend professional cleaning at least twice a year (spring and fall) to maintain glass integrity and curb appeal. Commercial properties often require monthly or quarterly service to maintain a professional appearance.

Do you clean screens and tracks?

Yes! We offer comprehensive cleaning that can include screens, tracks, frames, and sills. When requesting a quote, simply select "Screen Cleaning" or "Interior Windows" to ensure these areas are covered.

Do I need to be home for the service?

For exterior-only cleaning, you do not need to be home as long as we have access to all sides of the property. For interior cleaning, we require someone to be present to grant access.

Are your cleaning solutions safe?

Absolutely. We use eco-friendly, biodegradable cleaning solutions that are tough on dirt but safe for your family, pets, and landscaping. We treat your home with the utmost care.

Everything you need to know about our car detailing services

Do you come to my location?

Yes, we are a mobile detailing service! We come to your home or office with all the necessary equipment and supplies. We just need access to a water spigot and an electrical outlet.

How long does a full detail take?

A full interior and exterior detail typically takes anywhere between 2-5 hours, depending on the size and condition of the vehicle. Express services can be completed in 1-2 hours.

Can you remove pet hair?

Yes! We specialize in pet hair removal. While we can remove the vast majority of pet hair using specialized tools and techniques, please note that some stubborn fibers may remain depending on the fabric type.

What's the difference between a wash and a detail?

A car wash is a surface-level cleaning meant to remove loose dirt. A detail is a deep cleaning and reconditioning process that restores the vehicle's paint, wheels, seats, and carpets to near-perfect condition.
